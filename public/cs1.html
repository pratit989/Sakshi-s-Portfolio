App Design

Art Gallery Tour App

Designing an immersive tour experience

Project

Passion Project

My Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

8 weeks

Overview

As an artsy, I’ve always wondered what went through the artist’s brain while they created their artworks. What was their interpretation of it? What did they want to convey? These are the questions me and so many others ask when they are admiring an art piece.

“A picture is worth thousand words.”

But how will the people know what those words were. An audio guide would definitely be an answer to that. And thus I started my quest to create an App for Audio Tours. I took up this project as a part of Google’s UX Design Course. I’m more of an art enthusiast myself and from my first ever visit to an Art Gallery I felt like the experience could have been much more fulfilling. So I chose this prompt to create a compelling experience for Gallery visitors and myself.

Prompt :

Design a tour check-in app for an art gallery.

The Problem

Art Gallery visitors feel dissatisfied with the lack of information about artworks displayed. They lack structured tours.

The Product

Mobile Application

The Goal

To create a customized tour app for users with additional features to increase user engagement and make the experience immersive.

Design Process

I’ve presented the case study in a linear fashion but I follow a lean approach towards designing. Sometimes I brainstorm a few ideas before I’ve conducted user research. It helps me to enter the problem more naturally. Or sometimes even when I’ve completed the research and I’m still prototyping, I constantly look for any new insights from potential users. I believe the process of designing is never ending because there’s no ‘Perfect’ design.

User Research

Online Survey

I started the research with surveys to understand user perspective on Art Gallery Visits. I used Google Forms through which I was able to receive responses from art enthusiasts, frequent art gallery visitors, artists and some art gallery staff.

Survey Results

1:1 Interviews

The survey participants who were willing were interviewed. I interviewed 6 users from distinct backgrounds to get a comprehensive understanding of users. The interviews were semi-structured and included open-ended questions.

Key Quotes from Interview

“I keep reading every information card or boards but they only convey superficial facts and nothing about the painting or it’s artist. It feels bland.”

“The audio guides devices available are unhygienic and sometimes difficult to figure out. Usually most of the information is not available in my mother tongue.”

“Exploring all of the Art Gallery at once can be hectic and rushed due to time constraints.”

Competetive Analysis

What have the competitors been up to?

Key Research Insights

Users need deeper understanding about the artwork, its interpretation and the process.

Busy individuals want to get the best experience within a time restricted duration.

Most visitors worry about the hygiene when it comes to audio devices provided by the library.

Visitors find it irritating when the tour guide rushes them off.

It’s a challenge to get a good view of artwork when it’s crowded

Since some areas in Gallery have bad connection visitors aren’t able to google information about artwork.

Long booking process and queues are frustrating for users.

Define

What motivates the user?

Pain Points

I analyzed and synthesized the data found from the user research to define the pain points of users.

Inadequate information

Visitors not only crave about knowing the artwork information but also the artist’s interpretation.

No customized Tours

Some users prefer only specific genres of art while some have time constraints to explore the whole Gallery.

Navigation

Users get overwhelmed and get lost in the Gallery.

Accessibility Issues

Art Galleries struggle to be accessible.

Personas

I created two personas to reflect the pain points. These personas helped me to humanize the user throughout the process. It became easier to keep the user in mind before taking any design decision.

Problem Statement

Jeet is an Art Enthusiast who needs the experience in Art Gallery to be rewarding and inclusive because he feels left out during Gallery Tours.

Kruti is a busy professional who wants a customizable tour because she has limited time to visit the Art Gallery.

Goal

My goal was to create a customizable tour app which will provide structure to their visit but give them enough freedom to explore the Gallery at their own pace. The goal also included adding features to increase user engagement and make the experience immersive.
